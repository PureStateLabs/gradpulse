# Gradpulse Code of Conduct

Hey everyone! We want the Gradpulse community to be a welcoming, friendly, and safe place for everyone to learn, share, and contribute. To make sure that happens, we've put together a few guidelines on how we expect people to treat one another.

## Our Vibe

We're all here to build something great and learn from each other. That means treating everyone with respect, regardless of their background, experience level, gender, orientation, race, or anything else that makes them unique. 

We want this to be an inclusive space where people feel comfortable asking questions, sharing ideas, and giving feedback. 

## What We Expect

To keep our community healthy, we ask that you:
* **Be kind and empathetic.** Treat people the way you'd want to be treated.
* **Respect different viewpoints.** We don't all have to agree, but we do have to be respectful when we disagree.
* **Give and take feedback gracefully.** Constructive criticism is how we all get better. Keep it focused on the work, not the person.
* **Own your mistakes.** If you mess up or hurt someone's feelings, just apologize, learn from it, and move forward.

## What We Don't Tolerate

There are a few things that will absolutely not fly here:
* Harassment of any kind, whether it's public or private.
* Derogatory comments, personal attacks, insults, or trolling.
* Unwelcome sexual attention, language, or imagery.
* Doxing or sharing someone's private information without their permission.
* Any other behavior you wouldn't do in a professional setting.

## If Something Goes Wrong

If someone is making you or anyone else feel uncomfortable, or if you spot a violation of these guidelines, please let us know. You can report it directly to **info@purestatelabs.com**. 

We take all reports seriously and will review them promptly and confidentially. 

Depending on the situation, leadership might step in to give a warning, edit or remove inappropriate comments/code, or in serious or repeated cases, ban someone temporarily or permanently from the community. We want to be fair, but our top priority is keeping the community safe.

## Where This Applies

These guidelines apply everywhere the Gradpulse community hangs out—whether that's in our GitHub repository, issue trackers, forums, or any official events. It also applies if you're officially representing Gradpulse somewhere else online or offline.
